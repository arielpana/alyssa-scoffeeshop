Entities:

  1. Customer Attributes:
    Customer ID (Primary Key), Name, Contact Information

  2. Menu Item Attributes:
    Item ID (Primary Key), Name, Description, Price

  3. Order Attributes:
    Order ID (Primary Key), Date, Total Amount

  4. Employee Attributes:
    Employee ID (Primary Key), Name, Role, Contact Information

  5. Supplier Attributes:
    Supplier ID (Primary Key), Name, Contact Information

  6. Ingredient Attributes:
    Ingredient ID (Primary Key), Name, Stock Level, Unit of Measure

  7. Payment Attributes:
    Payment ID (Primary Key), Date, Amount

Relationships:

Customers place Orders (One-to-Many relationship between Customer and Order).
Orders consist of Menu Items (Many-to-Many relationship between Order and Menu Item, typically represented with a junction table).
Employees handle Orders (One-to-Many relationship between Employee and Order).
Suppliers provide Ingredients (One-to-Many relationship between Supplier and Ingredient).
Ingredients are used in Menu Items (Many-to-Many relationship between Ingredient and Menu Item, typically represented with a junction table).
Payments are associated with Orders (One-to-Many relationship between Payment and order.
